The demand for such a publication has snot been confined to Indians but also includes Europeans who have commercial and other contacts with Indians. Again its circulation is not only Union wide but a large number of copies go overseas, so that the world might know of South Africa’s Iudian commercial and professional personalities and public men. The fact that the size of the book, which is only in its second issue, has doubled itself is another proof that it has justified its existence, as well as its claim of meeting the need for an Indian reference publication for South Africa and the adjacent territories. We have wandered farther afield since the first issue and the result of our labours forms a valuable section of reference concerning Indians of Southern and Northern Rhodesia, Nyasaland and Portuguese East Africa.
